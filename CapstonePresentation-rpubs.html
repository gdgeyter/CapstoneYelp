Results and discussion

First Correlation is not causation. Therefore, one should ALWAYS we very careful when drawing conclusions from them. Because of this, and because of the smaller scale of this project, all the conclusions here will be expressed very conservativaly and should also be regarded as such.

When looking at previous slide we notice a few things:

  • Not suprisingly there is a high tendency for dietary restrictions to pair up.
  • Romantic users tend to be more upscale. This might not be so suprising as most people won't take their dates out on a cheap, fast-food restaurant.
  • Trendy people are more likely to be vegan and eat soy free.
  • Casual users are less likely to be classy, hispters or trendy.

Having this information could be an important tool for some businesses to adjust more to their clients needs. For example, using the second conclusion, upscale restaurants could focus specifically on advertising romantic dinners for two.

This is just a small example of the amount of information that could be locked in this data set alone so imagine what could be learned if it was enriched with other data like geospatial information of social network interests.
